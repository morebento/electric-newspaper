Monday June 22, 1998

SA - glowing all the way

South Australian shadow Environment Minister John Hill says he is not surprised that the Federal Government has announced that all 18 of its preferred sites for a nuclear waste repository are in SA, given the Olsen Government's seeming willingness to have the dump in our State.

"I am amazed that Environment Minister Dorothy Kotz showed absolutely no interest in whether or not Australia's nuclear waste is located in our State," Mr Hill said.

"When I asked if Ms Kotz was aware of the decision to close the Scottish nuclear waste reprocessing plant which was putting pressure on high-level waste being returned to our State, Ms Kotz said she wasn't even aware of it.

"Ms Kotz admitted she didn't know if the process for putting together an Environment Impact Statement had begun.

"When I asked Ms Kotz is she agreed with the site for nuclear waste being built in our State, Ms Kotz passed it off as being a Federal Government issue.

"When I asked if the Olsen Government has a policy in opposition to storing nuclear waste in South Australia, Ms Kotz said ‘No', there was not," Mr Hill said.
